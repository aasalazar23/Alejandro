Purpose

We wanted to take an anthropological perspective to understand the inspiration behind Hayao Miyazaki's animated film Spirited Away. Both of us have been fascinated by Studio Ghibli's films for the past decade and this was our opportunity to experience the film first hand.

Places

Miyazaki's films take place around the world. Spirited Away is no different. Though the setting of this film takes place in Japan, it draws from a number of elements from a small town in Taiwan called Jiufen. The main street is primarily a tourist attraction for people from Mainland China and contains various shops and food for visitors. It is most well known for the lanterns that hang across all the store fronts. This paired with the lush, mountainous terrain set the scene for our film. Despite this touch of Taiwanese culture, much of the architecture and the food is from Japan. For that reason the second leg of our trip took us there. We visited various museums to study the architecture of the Edo-Tokyo time period.

Symbols

Lanterns, Tea, Spirits, Dragons, Shrines, and Bathhouses were other topics we examined throughout the trip as they play a significant role in the film and in Taiwanese and Japanese culture. There's too much to discuss for this section, but we're hoping we can share more soon!
